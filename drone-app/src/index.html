About Drones

Welcome to the Drone Education App! Drones are uncrewed aircraft that can be controlled remotely or flown autonomously using pre-programmed software. They are used for a wide range of applications, from photography and videography to delivery and search-and-rescue operations.

The flight of a drone is controlled by a set of commands that are sent to its flight controller. These commands determine its altitude, speed, and direction. By understanding how to program these commands, you can make a drone fly a specific path or perform a complex task.

Key Components:

  • Flight Controller: The "brain" of the drone, responsible for processing inputs and controlling the motors.
  • Motors & Propellers: The components that generate thrust to lift and move the drone.
  • Battery: The power source for the drone.
  • Sensors: Components like GPS, gyroscopes, and accelerometers that provide data about the drone's position and orientation.

In this application, you will use Blockly, a visual programming language, to create sequences of commands for a simulated drone. Watch the simulation to see your code in action!
